Learn About Myopia Treatment

If your child is myopic, there are several options available to help slow down its progression. These should be discussed with your optometrist to see what's most suitable.

Specialty Contact Lenses

Daily disposable soft contact lenses are designed with special zones to control how light focuses in the eye, which can help reduce the signal for the eye to grow longer.

Orthokeratology (Ortho-K)

These are rigid gas-permeable contact lenses worn overnight to gently reshape the cornea. The lenses are removed in the morning, providing clear vision during the day without glasses or contacts.

Atropine Eye Drops

Low-dose atropine drops are used once a day to relax the eye's focusing mechanism. This has been shown to be effective in slowing down myopia progression, though glasses will still be needed for clear vision.

Spectacle Lenses for Myopia Management

These are advanced spectacle lenses that look like regular glasses but use special technology (like D.I.M.S. or H.A.L.T.) to manage how light is focused in the peripheral vision, helping to slow eye growth.

Why Regular Eye Tests Matter

A child's vision can change quickly. Regular eye tests are crucial to not only check for glasses but also to monitor the health of the eyes and detect signs of myopia progression early. Early detection is key to effective management.

Viewing Distance Game

Why Does Viewing Distance Matter?

When children hold devices or books too close for long periods, it puts extra strain on their eyes. This constant "close work" is a major risk factor for developing myopia (short-sightedness). Encouraging a healthy viewing distance helps their eyes relax and can significantly reduce the risk of myopia progressing.

This game shows what 'close work' is. Ask your child to hold the phone and tell you what picture they see. The app will check if they are holding it at a healthy distance.

Outdoor Activity Guide

Why Outdoors Matters

Natural daylight is crucial for healthy eye development in children. Aiming for at least 90 minutes of outdoor time a day can significantly reduce the risk of myopia progression.

Game: "I Spy" with a Twist!

Objective: Encourage kids to focus on distant objects.

How to Play:

  1. Find an open outdoor space like a park or garden.
  2. Player 1 starts by saying, "I spy with my little eye, something..." and then describes an object that is far away (e.g., "something green on that faraway hill").
  3. Other players have to guess the object.
  4. The person who guesses correctly gets to be the next "spy".

More Outdoor Ideas

  • Go on a nature scavenger hunt.
  • Fly a kite in an open field.
  • Play catch or frisbee.
  • Go for a bike ride as a family.
  • Visit a new playground you haven't been to before.
